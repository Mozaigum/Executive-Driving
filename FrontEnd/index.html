Executive Driving Services — Terms and Conditions (Alberta)

Last updated: 9/30/2025

1) Who we are and scope

These Terms and Conditions govern all executive or private SUV transportation and related concierge services provided by Executive Driving Services to any client or booking party. They apply to bookings made by phone, email, our website, and the Y Drive App. By placing a booking or riding with us, you accept these Terms.

2) Bookings, changes and substitutions

  • Accuracy. You are responsible for accurate pickup times, locations, passenger count, luggage, special needs such as child seats, and any access constraints such as gates or loading bays.
  • Verification. We may pre authorize a payment method before dispatch.
  • Vehicle substitution. For safety, maintenance, or operational reasons we may substitute a comparable or higher class vehicle at no extra cost. This does not give rise to any refund or credit unless the replacement materially fails to meet the booked capacity or specification.
  • Delays outside our control. Traffic, road closures, weather, law enforcement stops, and force majeure may affect arrival or ETA; we are not liable for consequential losses such as missed flights or meetings. See Limits of liability below.

3) Rates, waiting time and extras

  • Quoted rates cover vehicle, chauffeur, and standard distance or time as quoted or per hour as directed.
  • Waiting time. A fifteen minute grace at pickup; thereafter billed in fifteen minute increments at the hourly rate attached to your booking. Airport meet and greet may include a longer grace where stated.
  • Tolls, parking, and fees. Charged at cost where incurred.
  • After hours and peak surcharges may apply as disclosed at booking.
  • Overtime. If your service exceeds the booked duration, overtime is billed at the prevailing hourly rate.

4) Cancellations and no shows

Unless your confirmation states different terms:

  • Sedan or SUV point to point or hourly:
    • Free cancel or change at least twenty four hours before pickup.
    • Fifty percent charge if cancelled two to twenty four hours before pickup.
    • Full charge if cancelled under two hours before pickup or no show.
  • Special events, long distance, or multi day: seventy two hour free window; otherwise full charge once within seventy two hours.
  • No show means driver waits full grace period at the pickup point and cannot reach you on the phone or email or app on file.

You can tighten or loosen the windows above. These are industry standard starting points.

5) Passenger conduct and safety

  • Seatbelts. All occupants must wear seatbelts. Drivers must ensure minors are properly restrained; clients must arrange appropriate child restraints where required by law. Alberta law requires proper occupant restraint; children under six years and at most eighteen kilograms must be in an appropriate child safety seat.
  • Child seats. On request and if available, we can provide child seats for a stated fee. Ultimate compliance remains your responsibility.
  • No smoking or vaping. Smoking or vaping of any substance is prohibited in all vehicles.
  • Alcohol and cannabis. Open liquor must not be within easy reach of occupants, and consumption of cannabis is prohibited in moving vehicles. Keep any permitted cannabis sealed and out of reach. We may refuse carriage if rules are breached.
  • Pets and service animals. Service animals are welcome. Pets must be declared in advance and travel crated or as directed.
  • Right to refuse. We may refuse or terminate service with fees as applicable for unsafe, abusive, intoxicated, or unlawful conduct.

6) Cleaning, damage and downtime

To keep vehicles immaculate for all clients, the following cost recovery amounts apply where applicable. These are genuine pre estimates of likely costs such as detailing, materials, protective equipment, staff time, and downtime and are not penalties. We will document the condition with photos and invoices and adjust up or down to reflect actual, reasonable costs:

  • Standard detailing beyond normal wear: ninety five to one hundred fifty dollars.
  • Spills or stains requiring extraction: one hundred fifty to three hundred dollars.
  • Biohazard such as vomit or bodily fluids: three hundred to six hundred dollars plus downtime if vehicle is out of service.
  • Odour remediation for smoking or vaping: two hundred fifty to five hundred dollars plus remediation costs.
  • Damage, repairs, or replacement of interior or exterior parts: actual parts and labour plus reasonable downtime at your stated shop rate.

    • Receipts are provided where applicable. If you disagree, you may request an itemized review.

7) Payment, overdue amounts and chargebacks

  • Payment methods. We accept major credit cards, corporate invoicing if approved, Interac e Transfer, and cash. For security, card processing may be via a third party processor; we do not store full card numbers.
  • Pre authorization. We may place a pre authorization hold before dispatch.
  • Overdue amounts. Past due balances accrue interest at one point five percent per month or the maximum lawful rate, whichever is lower, plus reasonable collection and legal costs actually incurred in recovering the debt.
  • Interac e Transfers. Once a transfer is deposited, it generally cannot be reversed; disputes must be resolved between sender and recipient.
  • Unjust chargebacks. A $200 charge applies for any unjust chargeback, and all legal fees to collect will be billed to the client.
Seven point one friendly fraud or unjust chargebacks — seventy two hour cure

We take billing accuracy seriously and will work quickly to resolve any issue. Before initiating a card chargeback, you agree to notify us by phone, email, or app message and allow up to seventy two hours to investigate and propose a fix. If you still dispute the charge after our response, you may pursue a chargeback through your issuer.

  • If a chargeback is filed without first contacting us and the bank later determines it was unfounded such as you received the service as described, you agree we may invoice:
    • Our actual third party processor fees for the dispute, and
    • An administration or response cost recovery amount as a genuine pre estimate of time spent collecting trip records, GPS logs, and driver statements plus any proven external legal or collection costs actually incurred.
  • Seventy two hour cure with no fees. If you repay the disputed amount by cash or Interac e Transfer within seventy two hours of our first documented contact attempt about the dispute, we waive admin or collection fees and simply close the account paid in full.
  • Timelines. Banks and merchants operate under strict dispute timelines. Consumers typically have thirty to forty five days from statement date to dispute, and merchants must respond promptly. Contact us first. We resolve most issues faster than a bank dispute.

Important. Card network rules ultimately govern chargeback outcomes. We cannot contractually remove your right to dispute a charge. Our clause above is limited to documented cost recovery for unjustified chargebacks and does not penalize good faith disputes.

8) Lost property

We are not responsible for items left in vehicles, but we will help coordinate recovery when possible.

To the maximum extent permitted by law, our total liability for any claim is limited to the amount paid for the affected service. We are not liable for indirect, special, punitive, or consequential losses such as missed flights or meetings or lost profits. This clause does not exclude liability that cannot legally be limited under Alberta law. Alberta’s Consumer Protection Act construes ambiguous terms in the consumer’s favour; we have drafted plainly and fairly.

10) Dispute resolution, governing law and venue

These Terms are governed by the laws of Alberta. If you are a consumer, you may bring a claim in Alberta Small Claims or Court of Justice. Arbitration is optional by mutual agreement after a dispute arises; we do not require mandatory arbitration in consumer contracts. Forum selection and arbitration provisions in consumer agreements are restricted in Alberta; we follow those rules.

We may update these Terms from time to time. The version in effect at booking applies unless you agree to an update in writing or via the app.

Executive Driving Services — Privacy Policy Alberta PIPA

Last updated: 9/30/2025

1) About this Policy

We are committed to protecting your personal information in compliance with Alberta’s Personal Information Protection Act PIPA. This Policy explains what we collect, why, how we use or share it, where we store it, and your choices.

2) What we collect

  • Identity and contact such as name, company, phone, email.
  • Trip data such as pickup and drop off, dates and times, routing, special instructions.
  • Communications such as calls, emails, app messages.
  • Payment data such as tokenized card details via our processor. We do not store full card numbers and Interac e Transfer confirmations.
  • Device or app telemetry tied to bookings such as push tokens and crash logs.
  • Safety and compliance data such as incident reports, lost and found records, driver notes.

3) Purposes

We collect and use personal information to:

  • Accept, manage, and complete bookings; dispatch drivers; provide support.
  • Verify identity, prevent fraud or chargebacks; manage payments and invoicing.
  • Ensure safety and comply with traffic, seatbelt, child seat, and open liquor or cannabis rules.
  • Improve our services and app performance.
  • Communicate transactional notices; with your consent, send marketing per CASL. You can unsubscribe anytime.

4) Consent and your choices

We rely on your consent express or implied under PIPA for most collections and uses, and on other lawful grounds where permitted such as fraud prevention, fee collection, legal claims. You may withdraw consent, subject to legal or contractual limits and reasonable notice.

5) Sharing and cross border transfers

We share personal information with:

  • Payment processors and banking networks to process transactions and disputes.
  • Technology vendors such as hosting, email or SMS, app support under confidentiality agreements.
  • Law enforcement or regulators when legally required or to protect safety.

Some providers may store or process data outside Canada; that data may be subject to foreign laws. We use contractual and technical safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the information.

6) Security and retention

We use administrative, technical, and physical safeguards proportionate to the sensitivity of the data. We retain information only as long as reasonably necessary to provide services, meet legal or accounting requirements, and resolve disputes, then securely delete or de identify it.

7) Access, correction and complaints

You may request access to, or correction of, your personal information. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact the Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner of Alberta for a review or complaint.
